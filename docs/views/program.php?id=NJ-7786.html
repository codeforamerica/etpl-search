Description

Prerequisite: Candidates must obtain a reading score of 10 and math score of 10 on the Test of Adult Basic Education (TABE) or the Core Academic Skills Assessment (CASA). This program provides an introduction on how to build, maintain, support and troubleshoot computers. Students will learn the fundamentals of PC support and will acquire the necessary skills to obtain a CompTIA A+ certification. The concepts covered in this program include: ¿ Hardware: PC hardware and peripherals, mobile device hardware, networking and troubleshooting hardware and network connectivity issues; and, ¿ Software: installing and configuring operating systems including Windows, iOS, Android, Apple OS X and Linux. It also addresses security, the fundamentals of cloud computing and operational procedures. Students will receive two A+ test vouchers and two A+ practice tests.
